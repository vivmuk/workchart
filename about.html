From Org Chart to Work Chart

A modern model that maps how work actually gets done — by humans, AI agents, and hybrid processes — from trigger to outcome.

Key Ideas

  • Trigger → Outcome: Visualize complete workflows end-to-end.
  • Human + Agent Roles: Explicit owners for every step: Human, Agent, or Hybrid.
  • Success Targets: Measurable objectives for each step.
  • Rules: Governance and exceptions that guide execution.
  • Living Document: Continuously updated as workflows evolve.

The Evolution from "Org Chart" to "Work Chart"

Component Traditional Organization New AI-Driven Organization ("Work Chart" Model)
Fundamental Unit The Employee / Job Role: Static job description and title. The Task / Workflow: Smallest unit of outcome; roles are fluid and task-defined.
Structure Hierarchical pyramid; reporting lines and authority. Dynamic & networked map from trigger to outcome; linear or networked.
Team Composition Human-centric departments (e.g., Sales, Marketing). Hybrid squads of humans, AI agents, and hybrid processes aligned to goals.
Leadership & Management Managing people and job performance. Orchestrating work: "Work Stewards" manage interplay between humans and agents.
Primary Metric of Success Individual/team KPIs tied to functions (e.g., quotas, leads). Workflow throughput and efficiency: agent coverage ratio, human-in-the-loop rate, speed/quality of outcomes.
Career Progression Climbing the ladder to more authority and reports. Expanding skill and agent stack: manage more sophisticated agents and workflows.
Planning & Strategy Annual/quarterly plans; infrequent adjustments. Seasonal and adaptive planning; short 4–6 week squad sprints.
Product/Service Focus Product as a final artifact; periodic updates. Product as a living organism; continuous learning and management of its metabolism.
